Resolution, field by field: **game title/version are TRACKER-RESOLVED** — the backend now joins `game_artifacts`→`games`/`game_versions` on the announced content hash (`DISTINCT ON`, newest successful artifact) and returns `game_title`/`game_version`, which are **null for any hash this tracker has never indexed** (the normal case in a decentralized network, not an error); the lookup **fails soft** (a catalog query error logs and serves hashes, never fails the phonebook) because the authoritative half of the response is the signed ads. **Operator/region are NODE-DECLARED** — a tracker cannot verify where a box is or who runs it, so they became `Option<String>` fields *inside* the signed `SessionAd` body (covered by `signing_bytes`, so a relay cannot relabel someone else's box) and are rendered with a `self-declared` chip and a "signed by its key, but not verified by any tracker" tooltip. A phonebook reports what nodes claim; it does not certify it. **Hash form is plain hex everywhere** — `b3:` is gone from the frontend's data expectations; the `BLAKE3` chip is a rendering choice. Required a `Discovery::withdraw` default method (no-op default; `LanDiscovery` removes the slot, `TrackerDiscovery` delegates to a new `TrackerClient::withdraw` that the HTTP client wires to its existing *signed* `SignedWithdraw` call — previously that method existed but nothing reached it).
